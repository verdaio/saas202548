# Policy Guardrails

## Overview

This document outlines compliance requirements for all content produced for the saas202548 YouTube channel. Adherence to these guardrails protects the channel from policy violations, demonetization, and legal issues.

---

## YouTube Community Guidelines Compliance

### Prohibited Content (Never Create)

- **Hate speech**: Content promoting violence or hatred against individuals or groups
- **Harassment**: Targeting individuals with malicious intent
- **Dangerous content**: Instructing viewers to do harmful activities
- **Misinformation**: Demonstrably false claims about health, elections, etc.
- **Spam and deceptive practices**: Misleading metadata, artificial engagement
- **Sensitive content**: Graphic violence, adult content

### Advertiser-Friendly Guidelines

To maintain monetization eligibility:

| Category | Guideline |
|----------|-----------|
| Language | No excessive profanity; mild language sparingly |
| Violence | No graphic violence; educational context acceptable |
| Adult content | None; keep content suitable for general audiences |
| Drugs | No promotion; educational discussion with caution |
| Controversial topics | Present balanced views, no sensationalism |

## Copyright Compliance

### Music

- [ ] **Only use licensed music**
  - YouTube Audio Library (free, safe)
  - Epidemic Sound, Artlist, etc. (subscription, licensed)
  - Royalty-free with proper license
- [ ] **Never use** copyrighted music without explicit license
- [ ] **Document licenses** for all music used

### Images and Video

- [ ] Use original content or properly licensed stock
- [ ] Creative Commons: Follow attribution requirements exactly
- [ ] Fair use: Understand limitations; transform, don't just copy
- [ ] Screen recordings: Acceptable for educational commentary

### Software and Code

- [ ] Respect software licenses when showing code
- [ ] Don't reveal proprietary code without permission
- [ ] Attribute open-source projects appropriately

---

## Disclosure Requirements

### Affiliate Links

- [ ] Disclose affiliate relationships in video and description
- [ ] Use clear language: "This is an affiliate link" or "I may earn a commission"
- [ ] Don't hide disclosures in fine print

### Sponsorships

- [ ] Disclose paid sponsorships clearly at start of video
- [ ] Use YouTube's "Includes paid promotion" checkbox
- [ ] Don't make claims you can't verify about sponsors

### Product Ownership

- [ ] Disclose if you own or have equity in products you discuss
- [ ] Be transparent about free products received for review

---

## Claims and Guarantees

### Prohibited Claims

- **Medical claims**: "This will cure..." or "This is healthier than..."
- **Legal advice**: "You should definitely sue..." or "This is legal in all cases..."
- **Financial guarantees**: "You will make $X..." or "This is guaranteed to..."
- **Absolute statements**: "This is the only way..." or "This always works..."

### Acceptable Framing

- "In my experience..."
- "This worked for me because..."
- "You should consult a professional for..."
- "Results may vary; here's what I observed..."

---

## Source Attribution

### When to Cite

- Statistics and data points
- Quotes from other creators or publications
- Research findings
- Any claim that isn't common knowledge

### How to Cite

- Verbal mention in video: "According to [source]..."
- Description links: Include URLs to sources
- On-screen text: Source name for key stats
- End screen or pinned comment: Comprehensive source list

## Content Review Checklist

Before publishing any video, verify:

### Copyright
- [ ] All music is licensed or from YouTube Audio Library
- [ ] All images/video are original, licensed, or properly attributed
- [ ] Fair use principles followed if using third-party content

### Disclosures
- [ ] Affiliate links disclosed (if applicable)
- [ ] Sponsorships disclosed (if applicable)
- [ ] Product ownership disclosed (if applicable)

### Claims
- [ ] No medical, legal, or financial guarantees
- [ ] Strong claims are qualified ("in my experience", "typically")
- [ ] Sources cited for statistics and research

### Community Guidelines
- [ ] No hate speech or harassment
- [ ] No misleading metadata (title matches content)
- [ ] Advertiser-friendly (appropriate for general audiences)

### Attribution
- [ ] Sources linked in description
- [ ] Creators credited if featuring their work
- [ ] Open-source projects attributed

## Handling Violations

### If You Receive a Strike

1. Read the violation notice carefully
2. Understand which policy was violated
3. Decide: Appeal (if you believe it's wrong) or accept
4. Document the incident and resolution
5. Update processes to prevent recurrence

### If a Viewer Reports an Issue

1. Respond professionally and promptly
2. Investigate the claim
3. Correct or clarify if needed
4. Update content if there's a genuine issue
