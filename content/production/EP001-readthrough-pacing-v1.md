## Read-Through Procedure

### Pre-Read Setup:
1. **Print the script** (double-spaced, large font) OR display on large monitor
2. **Set up recording environment:**
   - Quiet room
   - Microphone positioned correctly
   - Water nearby
   - Timer/stopwatch ready
3. **Warm up:**
   - Read a few paragraphs out loud to settle into voice
   - Practice any tricky words or phrases (e.g., "On-Road Integrated Optimization and Navigation")

## Pacing Tips for Performance

### Speed Control:
- **Don't rush numbers** â€” listeners need time to absorb stats like "6.7 Ã— 10^198"
- **Pause after key facts** â€” let impactful statements breathe (e.g., "More routes than atoms in the universe.")
- **Slow down for complexity** â€” technical sections (how ORION works) should be slightly slower
- **Speed up for momentum** â€” narrative sections (building ORION, driver stories) can move faster

### Emphasis Patterns:
- **Numbers:** Say them clearly and with slight emphasis (e.g., "one HUNDRED million miles")
- **Key terms:** First mention of ORION, DIAD, traveling salesman problem â€” pronounce carefully
- **Contrast:** When comparing (left turn vs. right turn), use tone to differentiate

### Pauses:
- **Micro-pause (1 beat):** After commas, between clauses
- **Short pause (2 beats):** After sentences, before new thoughts
- **Medium pause (3-4 beats):** Between sections, after big reveals
- **Long pause (5+ beats):** After cold open (before Section 1), before CTA

### Energy Curve:
- **Cold Open:** High energy, hook the viewer
- **Section 1-2:** Moderate-high, build curiosity
- **Section 3:** Moderate, explain clearly (don't overwhelm)
- **Section 4:** Moderate-high, fun/surprising (right turn rule)
- **Section 5:** Warm, human connection ("Beat the Computer")
- **Section 6:** High, triumphant (results)
- **Recap:** Reflective, calm
- **CTA:** Warm, inviting (not salesy)

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### "The script feels too long, but I'm already at target word count"
**Cause:** Reading too slowly or adding unnecessary pauses
**Fix:** Try reading at 160 WPM (slightly brisker); trim filler words ("actually," "really," "just")

### "The script feels rushed"
**Cause:** Packing too much into a short time window
**Fix:** Identify 2-3 low-priority facts and remove them; prioritize clarity over completeness

### "I keep stumbling on the same phrase"
**Cause:** Awkward word order or tongue-twister
**Fix:** Rewrite the phrase â€” if you can't say it smoothly in 3 tries, the viewers won't parse it easily either

### "A section feels boring when I read it"
**Cause:** Too many stats in a row, or lack of narrative tension
**Fix:** Add a question, a human element, or a contrasting idea to break up monotony
