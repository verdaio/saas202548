## Recording Procedure

### Step 1: Warm-Up (5 minutes)
1. **Vocal warm-up:**
   - Hum scales or read a paragraph from another text out loud
   - Focus on clear enunciation and relaxed delivery
2. **Practice the first 30 seconds** (Cold Open) three times to settle into the rhythm
3. **Mental prep:** Remind yourself of the target delivery style (see below)

---

### Step 2: Full Read-Through (Do NOT Stop)

**Objective:** Read the entire script from start to finish at natural pace, as if recording for final production.

**Key Instructions:**
- **Do NOT stop for mistakes.** If you stumble, pause briefly and continue. Mark the issue on your script but keep going.
- **Aim for conversational delivery:** Calm, precise, but not robotic. Imagine explaining this to a curious friend.
- **Pause at section breaks:** 1-2 seconds between sections for breathing room.
- **Read numbers carefully:** "22 million" (pause) "packages," not "twenty-two-million-packages" (too fast).
- **Maintain energy:** Sections 1-2 should feel engaging; Section 4 (right turns) should feel like the "aha!" moment; Section 6 should feel triumphant but not overhyped.

**What to Mark During Read:**
- ‚≠ê **Stumbles:** Any word/phrase you restart, mispronounce, or trip over
- üü° **Awkward phrasing:** Sentences that feel clunky or unnatural
- üî¥ **Clarity issues:** Numbers or terms that feel unclear or rushed
- ‚è±Ô∏è **Pacing issues:** Sections that felt too fast or too slow

---

### Step 3: Immediate Post-Read Analysis (10 minutes)

**Do this IMMEDIATELY after finishing the read (while it's fresh):**

1. **Stop the recording and note the total time.**
2. **Listen back to the first 2 minutes** (Cold Open + Section 1 start).
   - Did the hook feel engaging?
   - Were the numbers clear?
   - Any pacing issues?
3. **Listen back to Section 4** (Right Turn Rule, ~11:40-14:30).

## Target Delivery Style

**Tone:** Calm, curious, precise
- **NOT:** Overly enthusiastic, "YouTube voice," or promotional
- **YES:** NPR/podcast style, conversational but polished

**Pacing:** Deliberate without dragging
- **Target:** 150 WPM average
- **Slow down for:** Numbers, key terms (ORION, traveling salesman problem), pattern interrupts (questions)
- **Speed up slightly for:** Transitions, recap sections (if they feel natural)

**Energy Curve:**
- **Cold Open:** Medium-high (hook the viewer)
- **Section 1:** Medium (establish stakes)
- **Section 2-3:** Medium (building context)
- **Section 4:** Medium-high (the "aha!" moment ‚Äî right turns)
- **Section 5:** Medium (human story)
- **Section 6:** Medium-high (results)
- **Section 7 + CTA:** Medium (warm close, no hard sell)

**Pauses:**
- After questions: 1 beat (e.g., "What does this look like in practice?" ‚Üí pause ‚Üí "Let's say you're a driver in Atlanta...")
- After big stats: 1 beat (e.g., "6.7 √ó 10^198 possible routes" ‚Üí pause ‚Üí "That's more combinations...")
- Between sections: 2 beats

## Common Issues and Fixes

### Issue: Running Too Long (>16:30)
**Likely Causes:**
- Pausing too long between sentences
- Over-enunciating or speaking too slowly
- Adding ad-libs or extra words

**Fixes:**
- Tighten pauses (1 beat instead of 2)
- Read at slightly faster pace (test in 30-second bursts)
- If still over, apply Tier 1 cuts (see Pacing v2 for cut list)

### Issue: Running Too Short (<15:00)
**Likely Causes:**
- Rushing through sentences
- Skipping pauses or breathing points
- Swallowing numbers or key terms

**Fixes:**
- Slow down delivery, especially on numbers
- Add deliberate pauses after questions and key stats
- Re-read sections that felt rushed

### Issue: Stumbles on Specific Sections
**Common Trouble Spots (from Script v2 analysis):**
- Section 1, Line 58: "6.7 √ó 10^198" (practice saying "six point seven times ten to the one hundred ninety-eighth")
- Section 4, Line 201-203: Mythbusters reference (long sentence ‚Äî break into two breaths)
- Section 6, Line 268: Multiple stats in sequence (slow down, pause between each)

**Fix:**
- Mark the line in your script
- Practice the phrase 3-5 times before re-recording
- If still awkward, flag for script edit

### Issue: Energy Dip Mid-Video
**Common Dip Zone:** Section 3 (How ORION Works, ~8:00-11:30)
**Fix:**
- Take a 30-second break before Section 3
- Drink water
- Re-read the section with slightly more energy (as if explaining to someone who's genuinely curious)
