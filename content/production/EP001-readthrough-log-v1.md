## Top 3 Retention Risks

### 1. Number Overload (Section 1 + Section 6)
**Issue:** Too many stats in rapid succession without context or breathing room
**Impact:** Viewers tune out when overwhelmed; numbers blur together
**Fix:** Cut non-essential stats, add narrative between numbers, visualize more

### 2. Abstract Concepts (Section 1: Factorial/Atoms, Section 3: Technical How-It-Works)
**Issue:** Traveling salesman problem and combinatorics are hard to grasp; ORION's technical workings are jargon-heavy
**Impact:** Viewers may feel lost or intimidated
**Fix:** Add concrete analogy for factorial explosion; simplify technical section with everyday language

### 3. Mid-Video Energy Dip (Section 3, 8:00-11:30)
**Issue:** Long expository section with no narrative tension, questions, or pattern interrupts
**Impact:** Viewers drop off at 8-12 minute mark (common YouTube pattern)
**Fix:** Insert question ("What does this look like in practice?"), concrete example, or mini-story
